# Annotation Guidelines

## Human Labels vs Controlled Business Pseudo-Labels

Human labels are judgments provided by a recruiter, evaluator, or domain expert after reading the job offer and candidate evidence.

Controlled business pseudo-labels are automatic labels generated by transparent rules. They are useful for testing the ML pipeline, but they are not recruiter labels and must not be presented as ground truth.

## Label Grid

Label 3 = very relevant:
- Strong coverage of required skills.
- Very few critical missing skills.
- Experience aligned with the job.
- Seniority aligned or acceptable.
- Reliable profile.
- Low hallucination risk.

Label 2 = relevant:
- Good global profile.
- Most required skills covered.
- Acceptable experience.
- Some non-blocking limitations.

Label 1 = weak:
- Some positive signals.
- Several important gaps.
- Experience or seniority may be weak.

Label 0 = not relevant:
- Most required skills are missing.
- Different domain.
- Insufficient experience.
- Seniority very far from the job need.

## Important Rule

`final_score_v3`, `rank`, and `must_have_coverage` may help the annotator inspect the case, but they must not be copied directly as labels.
